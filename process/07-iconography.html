Recommendation: Phosphor Light (B)

  • Light → Fill — Native active state pattern. No hacks. One library, two clear visual states.
  • Glass aesthetic — The ~1px stroke harmonizes with glass borders (4-6% white). Tabler's 2px visually dominates the subtle glass surfaces.
  • Premium differentiation — Neither Lucide (ubiquitous) nor Tabler (dashboard-template feel) differentiate Rexi. Phosphor Light feels refined and distinctive.
  • shadcn/ui reality check — Tabler does NOT have native shadcn support either. Both Tabler and Phosphor require the same hybrid approach (Lucide for shadcn internals, your library for app code). There is no advantage to Tabler here.
  • Bundle — Tabler has documented Next.js bundle issues. Phosphor is clean.

Why Not Tabler?

  • 2px stroke fights the glass system — Your borders are 4-6% white opacity. A 2px icon stroke has more visual weight than the border itself, breaking the hierarchy.
  • No native shadcn/ui support — Despite common belief, Tabler is NOT supported in shadcn's iconLibrary config. Same hybrid approach needed as Phosphor.
  • Bundle size issues — Documented problems with Next.js/Vite builds (5-10MB bloat in development).
  • Aesthetic mismatch — Tabler's geometric, utilitarian feel is designed for admin panels, not premium glass morphism UIs.
